# LetsGrow Landing Page Messaging Brief

## Document Purpose

This document is the source of truth for the LetsGrow launch website messaging.

It is written in English for internal clarity, but the launch website itself should be written in Danish.

The purpose of the site is not to describe every possible future use case. Its job is to clearly position LetsGrow for a narrow, credible launch wedge: helping Danish team leads improve recurring meetings through lightweight, automatic feedback.

---

## Executive Positioning

### Category

Recurring meeting improvement software.

### Who It Is For

Danish team leads, managers, scrum masters, and other meeting owners who run recurring meetings.

### Core Outcome

Better recurring meetings that become measurable and easier to improve over time, with almost no setup.

### Positioning Statement

LetsGrow helps Danish team leads, scrum masters, and other meeting owners improve recurring meetings through automatic micro-feedback embedded in their existing meeting workflow.

### Positioning Guardrail

LetsGrow should not be positioned as a general feedback platform, a survey tool, or a broad self-improvement product.

---

## Vision

LetsGrow exists to make recurring meetings continuously improvable.

The core belief is simple:

- What repeats can be measured.
- What can be measured can improve.
- Recurring meetings should not run on habit alone.

LetsGrow creates a lightweight feedback loop around meetings teams already run. It does not ask teams to replace their calendar workflow, adopt a heavy process, or launch a large internal program. It adds a small, repeatable signal that helps team leads see whether their meetings are getting better, staying flat, or getting worse.

This is an operational product vision, not a broad culture vision. The launch message should stay anchored in recurring meeting improvement.

---

## Problem Statement

Team leads spend a meaningful share of their week in recurring meetings, but most of those meetings are not actively improved.

The common pattern is:

- the meeting repeats every week
- the format stays mostly the same
- people have opinions about whether it is useful
- nobody captures those opinions in a consistent way

As a result, team leads do not know whether their recurring meetings are becoming more useful, more focused, or more energizing over time.

This problem is especially visible in common Danish knowledge-work settings such as:

- weekly team meetings
- 1:1s
- sprint reviews
- leadership check-ins

Traditional surveys are too slow, too heavy, and too disconnected from the meeting itself. Teams need a small feedback habit that happens immediately after the meeting and becomes useful because it repeats over time.

## Danish-First Go-To-Market Strategy

LetsGrow should launch as a Danish website first.

This is a strategic focus choice, not just a translation choice.

### Why Danish First

- Local-language trust matters more than early international breadth.
- Danish-first copy makes the product feel specific, practical, and credible.
- The launch site should sound like it understands how Danish teams actually work.
- A narrow local wedge is more believable than a broad global SaaS message.

### Language Strategy

- Internal strategy brief: English
- Website UI and marketing copy: Danish
- Product examples in this brief: include Danish examples where useful

### Strategic Implication

The site should avoid generic international SaaS phrasing. It should speak in plain Danish about recurring meetings, team habits, meeting quality, and low-friction improvement.

English can come later, once positioning and conversion are validated.

## Core Message Architecture

The website should be built around three core messaging pillars.

### 1. Better Recurring Meetings

#### Customer Belief It Supports

Recurring meetings take a lot of time and should get better over time, not just repeat.

#### Proof Mechanism

LetsGrow measures recurring meeting quality with repeatable post-meeting feedback.

#### Copy Direction

Use copy that connects meeting repetition to improvement.

Example Danish direction:

- `Jeres faste møder skal blive bedre, ikke bare gentage sig.`
- `Når et møde sker hver uge, skal kvaliteten kunne følges.`

### 2. Feedback With Zero Friction

#### Customer Belief It Supports

Teams will only adopt feedback if it fits into the workflow they already have.

#### Proof Mechanism

The meeting is created as usual, `feedback@letsgrow.dk` is invited, and participants answer 2-3 short questions after the meeting.

#### Copy Direction

Emphasize that there is no heavy setup, no long survey, and no separate process to maintain.

Example Danish direction:

- `Inviter feedback@letsgrow.dk og kom i gang uden tung opsætning.`
- `Ingen lange spørgeskemaer. Kun få spørgsmål efter mødet.`

### 3. See Improvement Over Time

#### Customer Belief It Supports

Feedback becomes valuable when it reveals a pattern, not just a one-off opinion.

#### Proof Mechanism

LetsGrow turns repeated responses into a meeting quality score and visible trend development over time.

#### Copy Direction

Use copy that shows progress, stability, or decline as the core benefit.

Example Danish direction:

- `Se om jeres møder bliver bedre uge for uge.`
- `Få et tydeligt billede af, om kvaliteten stiger, står stille eller falder.`

## Website Narrative Structure

The homepage should follow a clear narrative arc. Each section needs a defined job.

### 1. Hero

#### Job

State who the product is for, what it improves, and why it matters.

#### Requirements

- speak to team leads and meeting owners
- stay concrete
- avoid vague “growth” language
- make the recurring-meeting wedge obvious

#### Example Danish Hero Directions

- `Gør jeres møder bedre, uge for uge`
- `Få enkel feedback efter hvert møde og se, om kvaliteten stiger`
- `Bedre teammøder starter med en lille vane`

### 2. Problem

#### Job

Make the cost of weak recurring meetings feel immediate and recognizable.

#### Key Message

Recurring meetings consume time, but most teams do not improve them systematically.

### 3. How It Works

#### Job

Show the workflow in simple terms.

#### Core Flow

1. Create your meeting as usual.
2. Invite `feedback@letsgrow.dk`.
3. LetsGrow handles the post-meeting feedback automatically.
4. Participants answer 2-3 short questions after the meeting.
5. LetsGrow tracks meeting quality over time.

### 4. Why Recurring Meetings Are The Wedge

#### Job

Explain why LetsGrow starts here and why the use case is strong.

#### Key Message

Meetings that repeat create the conditions for measurable improvement. One-off feedback is weak. Repeated feedback creates direction.

### 5. Proof / Outcomes

#### Job

Translate the product into visible outcomes for the buyer.

#### Outcome Areas

- better clarity
- better energy
- better usefulness
- faster identification of weak meeting formats

### 6. FAQ / Objections

#### Job

Handle friction before it blocks conversion.

#### Likely Questions To Address

- Do we need to change our current calendar workflow?
- Is this a long survey?
- Is this only for big companies?
- What kinds of meetings work best?

### 7. CTA

#### Job

Turn interest into a clear next step.

#### CTA Strategy

- Primary CTA: `Få tidlig adgang`
- Secondary CTA: `Se hvordan det virker` if a supporting explanation section exists
- Primary CTA should link directly to a Typeform
- Support flow: short Typeform intake followed by manual follow-up or invite
- Avoid making App Store download the main CTA while onboarding and pricing are still being shaped
- Avoid adding `Book demo` unless the operating model and follow-up process support it

## Strategic Boundaries

### What We Are

- a recurring meeting improvement product
- a lightweight feedback layer around existing meetings
- an operational tool for team leads, scrum masters, and meeting owners

### What We Are Not

- a general survey platform
- an HR suite
- a generic productivity dashboard
- a personal development app
- a broad feedback tool for any use case

### Boundary Reminder For Website Messaging

The current broad positioning around “meetings or personal skills” is off-strategy for launch. The website should stay tightly focused on recurring meetings.

---

## Product Strategy Sequence

The product roadmap can remain ambitious, but the launch message must stay narrow.

### Phase 1: Launch Wedge

- recurring meeting feedback
- a default fallback template, with support for custom templates
- meeting quality score
- trend tracking over time

### Phase 2: Expanded Utility

- recommendations for improving specific meeting types
- participation consistency tracking
- team-level insight summaries

### Phase 3: Broader Operating Signals

- broader team operating system signals
- cross-team benchmarking, only when enough data quality and trust exist

### Strategic Rule

Phase 3 can exist in the roadmap, but it must not shape the launch positioning. The homepage should sell Phase 1 clearly and credibly.
