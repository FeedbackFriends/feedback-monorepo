# LetsGrow Pricing, Business, and Account Strategy

## Purpose

This document proposes a practical commercial strategy for LetsGrow based on the current landing page positioning:

- recurring meeting improvement software
- Danish-first launch
- lightweight automatic feedback
- simple setup around existing meeting workflows

The goal is to make pricing, account creation, and the early business model simple enough to launch without adding unnecessary friction.

It also needs to stay aligned with the landing page brief: recurring-meeting focus, low-friction setup, and an early-access CTA while the commercial model is still being validated.

---

## Recommended Commercial Direction

LetsGrow should launch as a focused B2B SaaS product for team leads and meeting owners, not as a broad employee feedback platform.

The commercial model should follow this logic:

- land with one team lead or meeting owner
- prove value on a small number of recurring meetings
- expand to more meeting owners inside the same company
- later expand to department or company-wide usage

This means the first version of the business model should optimize for easy adoption, clear value, and low buying friction.

---

## What LetsGrow Should Sell

LetsGrow is not selling "more survey responses."

LetsGrow is selling:

- better recurring meetings
- less wasted meeting time
- visible improvement over time
- a lightweight habit for team leads

That matters because pricing should reflect operational value, not generic SaaS logic.

---

## Recommended Pricing Model

### Recommendation

Start with owner-based pricing inside one workspace subscription, not per participant and not per company-wide seat.

That is the cleanest fit with the current positioning because:

- the buyer is the team lead, manager, or scrum master
- the product value is tied to the person responsible for improving the meeting
- participant-based pricing will feel expensive too fast
- company-wide pricing is too early before expansion patterns are proven

### Why Not Price Per Participant

Do not price per participant in the early phase.

It creates the wrong incentives:

- customers hesitate to include everyone
- cost becomes hard to predict
- the product starts to feel like a survey tool

### Why Not Price Per Meeting

Pricing per recurring meeting is possible, but it is harder to understand and sell.

Team leads think in terms of ownership, not in terms of counting every single recurring event. A meeting-owner model is easier to explain and easier to expand later.

## Suggested Launch Pricing

### Phase 1: Design Partner

Use this now if the product is still being shaped.

- Price: free
- Length: 6-8 weeks
- Target: 3-5 teams
- Requirement: active usage, feedback calls, permission to use learnings in future sales material

Purpose:

- validate onboarding
- validate whether teams keep using it after the first few meetings
- learn what buyers actually perceive as value

### Phase 2: Early Access Paid

Use this once onboarding works and teams can get value without heavy manual support.

- `Starter`: 499 DKK/month per workspace
- Includes: 1 meeting owner, up to 3 recurring meetings, and up to 25 participants total
- Extra meeting owner: 299 DKK/month
- Annual option: 4,990 DKK/year

Why this is a good starting point:

- low enough for card-based purchase
- high enough to signal business value
- simple enough to explain on a landing page

### Phase 3: Standard Self-Serve

Use this after you have early evidence of retention and buyer willingness to pay.

- `Starter`: 499 DKK/month for 1 meeting owner
- `Team`: 1,499 DKK/month for up to 5 meeting owners
- `Business`: custom pricing for larger teams, procurement needs, or advanced reporting

At that point, the business plan can include:

- better insights dashboards
- admin controls
- department roll-up reporting
- invoicing

## Recommended Pricing Principles

These principles should guide pricing decisions:

### 1. Keep The First Purchase Small

The first purchase should feel like:

"Let us try this on our weekly team meeting."

It should not feel like:

"We need company approval for a new HR platform."

### 2. Charge For Owner Value, Not Employee Reach

The economic buyer is the meeting owner. That is where the value begins.

### 3. Do Not Create Too Many Plans

At launch, one paid plan is enough.

If you add more than that too early, you create internal complexity before you have enough data.

### 4. Do Not Require Annual Contracts Early

Offer annual as an option later, but monthly should be the default in the beginning.

### 5. Make Expansion Obvious

The natural upsell is:

- more meeting owners
- more teams
- better reporting

That should be more important than adding complex feature gates.

## Recommended Business Model

### Core Model

LetsGrow should begin as a low-touch B2B SaaS product with optional founder-assisted onboarding.

The model should be:

- Typeform-based early-access signup from the landing page
- light qualification and guided setup for early users
- optional founder support for selected early teams
- recurring monthly subscription once value is proven

### Primary Buyer

- team leads
- managers
- scrum masters
- department leads with recurring team rituals

### Expansion Buyer Later

- head of engineering
- operations leads
- people managers across multiple teams

### Best Initial Customer Profile

Start with companies that:

- already run recurring meetings consistently
- are small or midsize
- can buy without long procurement cycles
- have one visible meeting owner who cares about improvement

Avoid optimizing first for:

- enterprise HR
- company-wide engagement programs
- procurement-heavy sales cycles

## Recommended Activation Strategy

The key activation event should not be "user created account."

The key activation event should be:

"A team lead has set up one real recurring meeting and received the first round of feedback."

That means the activation funnel should be measured like this:

1. landing page visit
2. Typeform early-access form submitted
3. workspace created
4. first recurring meeting configured
5. first participant responses received
6. second recurring meeting cycle completed
7. paid conversion

If teams do not reach step 5 or 6, pricing is not the main problem. Onboarding or product clarity is.

## When To Ask For Payment

### Recommendation

Do not ask for card details before the team sees the first real value.

The best early model is:

- free pilot or founder-led setup
- then convert to paid once the team has at least one active recurring meeting and visible feedback data

### Pilot Payment Setup

In pilot phase, keep payment extremely simple:

- no credit card upfront
- no self-serve checkout
- no automated seat billing

Recommended default:

- pilot is free for 6-8 weeks
- workspace is created manually
- one primary contact is responsible for the pilot

### If You Want Paid Pilots

If some pilots should pay, keep it manual:

- invoice manually
- use one fixed monthly or pilot fee
- avoid building a full billing flow yet

### Later Trial Option

Once self-serve is stable, offer:

- 14-day free trial
- no credit card upfront

This fits the current product promise better than a hard paywall at signup.

### Payment Model After Pilot

Once pilot converts into paid early access, the billing model should be:

- one subscription per workspace
- billed to the workspace owner
- price based on how many meeting owners are active in that workspace

That means the payer can add meeting owners, and the monthly price adjusts accordingly.

Example:

- 1 meeting owner = `499 DKK/month`
- extra meeting owner = add-on price

This is better than forcing each meeting owner to buy separately.

### Recommended Billing Logic

Keep the logic simple:

- the workspace owner starts the subscription
- the workspace includes one paid meeting owner by default
- adding another meeting owner increases the subscription
- removing a meeting owner reduces the price on the next billing cycle

That is enough structure for early access without overbuilding.

## Recommended Landing Page Business Offer

The landing page should communicate a very simple offer that matches the messaging brief.

Suggested launch framing:

- For Danish team leads and meeting owners
- Improve recurring meetings with automatic feedback
- Invite `feedback@letsgrow.dk` and follow meeting quality over time
- Primary CTA: `Få tidlig adgang`
- CTA destination: Typeform
- Secondary CTA: `Se hvordan det virker`

If LetsGrow is still in a selective design-partner phase, add a small supporting note such as:

- Limited pilot spots for selected teams

If pricing is still uncertain, do not put a full public pricing table on the homepage yet.

Instead, keep the commercial message at this level:

- simple setup
- for recurring meetings
- for Danish team leads and meeting owners
- early access for selected teams

Do not make `Book demo`, `Talk to sales`, App Store download, or an upfront pricing table the primary homepage action unless the operating model actually supports that motion.

---

## What You Should Avoid

Avoid these decisions in the early phase:

- pricing per employee
- requiring every participant to create a login
- free plan with no clear upgrade path
- enterprise-style pricing pages with many tiers
- charging before users have seen actual meeting trend data
- broad positioning as a general feedback platform

These choices would weaken the current wedge.

---

## Recommended Default Strategy For LetsGrow Right Now

If a decision had to be made today, the recommended strategy would be:

- Position LetsGrow as a B2B SaaS tool for improving recurring meetings
- Sell first to individual meeting owners and small teams
- Use `Få tidlig adgang` as the public CTA
- Send that CTA to a Typeform
- Use founder-led support only for selected early teams
- Keep participants account-free
- Keep pricing semi-private until the offer is validated
- Internally test one simple workspace plan at `499 DKK/month` including 1 meeting owner
- Charge `299 DKK/month` for each extra meeting owner if expansion happens inside the same workspace
- Offer free pilot access to the first few design partners
- Move to self-serve only after activation and retention are visible

This is the simplest path that matches the current landing page and reduces commercial risk.

## Decisions To Make Next

The next concrete choices for LetsGrow should be:

1. Decide whether the next 2-3 months are `pilot phase` or `paid early access phase`
2. Decide whether `Få tidlig adgang` or `Bliv pilotkunde` is the final public CTA copy
3. Decide the exact included limits in the launch plan:
   - number of recurring meetings
   - number of participants
   - number of meeting owners
4. Decide when a team moves from free to paid
5. Define the minimum activation metric before scaling acquisition
6. Decide whether the first paid model is:
   - one included meeting owner plus add-ons
   - or a team tier after the first user
7. Decide when workspace owners are allowed to add more meeting owners without manual approval

---

## Strong Recommendation

Do not optimize for perfect pricing yet.

Optimize for:

- repeated usage
- a clear activation event
- one buyer
- one simple paid offer

If teams keep using LetsGrow for recurring meetings, pricing can be adjusted later. If they do not, more pricing complexity will not help.
